Assumptions & Scope

  • All figures are material cost only — no labor, no GC markup
  • Barndominium shell = post-frame steel kit (metal roof, metal siding, trusses, purlins, overhead doors)
  • PNW seismic zone D requirements (+10–15%) are reflected in framing and foundation rates
  • WA State Energy Code (2021) compliance assumed — affects insulation and window specs
  • 2-story: same roof footprint; second floor framed with LVL beams and engineered lumber
  • Garage: attached under the same roof, slab-on-grade regardless of main foundation type
  • Window count: 1 per 100 sqft living area + egress per bedroom + 1 per bath (min 4)
  • Interior doors: 1 per bedroom + 1 per bath + 3 misc (pantry, utility, closets)
  • Kitchen covers cabinets, countertops, sink/faucet, and a standard appliance package
  • Plumbing includes rough-in pipe, fixtures, and water heater; no water softener or RO
  • Electrical includes panel, meter base, all branch circuits, devices, and fixture rough-in
  • HVAC sized at 1 ton per 500 sqft; actual load calc may differ for high-ceiling barndos
  • Well: 300–400 ft drilled, submersible pump, pressure tank ($25k avg)
  • Septic: conventional gravity system, 3–4 BR size ($22k avg)
  • Site work excludes permits, soil engineering, and utility trenching to street
  • Driveway, fencing, landscaping, and outbuildings are not included
  • Prices sourced from PNW supplier quotes, RS Means, and regional builder surveys (2025–2026)
⚠ Labor multipliers by trade (materials × this): Framing 1.8× · Concrete 2.0× · Electrical 2.5× · Plumbing 2.5× · HVAC 2.0× · Finish carpentry 1.6× · Painting 1.5×. For a rough all-in budget, multiply this estimate by 2.0–2.5× then add permits and engineering.
